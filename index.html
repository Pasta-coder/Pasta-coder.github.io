About

I'm a second-year Computer Science student at Netaji Subhas University of Technology (NSUT), New Delhi — admitted through JEE Main (99.22 percentile) and JEE Advanced (AIR 10,527).

Most of my time goes into two things: writing code that ships into large open-source codebases, and building ML systems that solve real problems. On the open-source side, I contribute to the GCC Rust frontend — fixing internal compiler errors, building AST/HIR reconstruction logic, and extending pattern matching infrastructure. I also contributed to GNU Octave's statistics package, where I rewrote the stepwise regression backend and implemented one-hot encoding from scratch.

On the applied side, I've built SatyaSetu.AI, a forensic analysis platform that combines OCR, NER, and ML-based fraud detection — trained on 1.8M+ procurement contracts and recognised in the Top 30 at India Innovates 2026. I also led a team to build VividReel, an AI video summarisation pipeline that won a hackathon as the youngest competing team.

When I'm not coding, I volunteer with PRAYAS at NSUT, mentoring underprivileged students in math and science.
